About the Program

Cameras Save Lives

Background, history, and effectiveness of Victoria's Road Safety Camera Program. Sources: camerassavelives.vic.gov.au, vic.gov.au, cameracommissioner.vic.gov.au.

Program Purpose: Victoria's Road Safety Camera Program uses automated camera technology to detect and deter traffic offences — primarily speeding and red-light running — which are major contributors to road trauma. The program is administered by the Department of Justice and Community Safety and overseen by the Office of the Road Safety Camera Commissioner, established in 2012.
📷

Fixed Intersection Cameras

Permanently installed at signalised intersections and on freeways. Can detect speed, red-light running, and unregistered vehicle offences simultaneously.

  • Speed captured twice using 2 independent measurement units
  • Images encrypted and stored electronically
  • Two officers must independently agree an offence occurred
  • 47% reduction in casualty crashes at installation sites
  • 26% reduction in crashes on all approaches to the intersection
  • 44% reduction in right-angle (T-bone) crashes
  • 287 fixed sites across Victoria (January 2023 data)
🚗

Mobile Speed Cameras

Camera vehicles and trailers operated by Victoria Police highway patrols. Deployed at approved high-risk locations across the state.

  • Radar-based detection — measures speed and direction of travel
  • Can detect speeding in one or both directions
  • 9,300 camera hours rostered per month across ~2,000 sites
  • Covert and overt deployments
  • Gatso T-Series camera system (operational from April 2023)
  • Sites selected based on speed history, crashes, complaints, school proximity
  • 2,242 approved locations as of March 2026
🛣️

Highway (Point-to-Point) Cameras

Fixed speed cameras on 7 major freeway corridors. CityLink, EastLink, Geelong Rd, Hume, Monash, Peninsula Link and Western Ring Road.

  • Instantaneous speed detection at camera location
  • P2P cameras on Hume Freeway and Peninsula Link calculate average speed across zones
  • Peninsula Link: 25 cameras at 8 locations over 27 km
  • Creates 3 northbound and 3 southbound P2P zones on Peninsula Link
  • Proven measurable benefit to road safety
  • $49.4M invested in 35 new fixed digital cameras + 2 new P2P networks
📱

Distracted Driver & Seatbelt (DDS) Cameras

Trailer-mounted AI-assisted cameras detecting mobile phone use and seatbelt non-compliance. Deployed across Victoria since 2023.

  • Each trailer: 2 cameras + infrared flash — works day and night
  • Forward-facing camera: detects phone-to-ear and seatbelt offences
  • Downward-facing camera: detects phone resting on body/touching
  • AI screens all images — flags potential offences for human review
  • No facial recognition technology used
  • Victoria Police issue all fines and demerit points
  • $156.1M investment — program runs to 2030
  • Predicted to prevent 95 casualty crashes per year
  • 344 approved locations as of March 2026

Oversight & Governance

Road Safety Camera Commissioner
Independent office established 2012. Reviews camera operations, audits accuracy, investigates complaints, and reports publicly. Website: cameracommissioner.vic.gov.au

Department of Justice & Community Safety
Administers the overall camera program, manages contracts with camera operators, and publishes statistical data at data.vic.gov.au.

Victoria Police
Operates mobile camera vehicles. Issues all infringement notices. Determines mobile camera deployment locations and hours.

Fines Victoria
Manages infringement notice processing, payment, and enforcement under the Fines Reform Act.

How It Works

Legislation & Process

The infringement process, legislation, fine amounts and demerit points for Victoria road safety camera offences.

Infringement Process

1

Offence Detected

Camera captures images of the vehicle. For speed, detection occurs twice using 2 independent units. Two authorised officers must independently agree an offence has occurred.

2

Image Review & Certification

Encrypted images are reviewed by authorised personnel. Camera test certificates can be admitted as evidence the camera was operating correctly. DDS images reviewed by trained human operators after AI pre-screening.

3

Infringement Notice Issued

An Infringement Notice is issued to the registered operator of the vehicle (typically by mail). Contains fine amount, due date, offence details, and how to pay or contest.

4

Payment or Dispute (21 days)

The recipient has 21 days to pay, nominate another driver, request a hearing, or apply for an extension. Ignoring the notice leads to escalating consequences.

5

Penalty Reminder Notice

If unpaid, a Penalty Reminder Notice is issued with a late fee added. Then a Notice of Final Demand (another fee). Continued non-payment triggers an Enforcement Warrant — the Sheriff may become involved.

Key Evidence Rules

Under the Road Safety Act 1986, photos and data captured by certified road safety cameras are admissible as evidence of a speeding or red-light offence. Camera test certificates may be tendered in court to prove the camera was calibrated and operating correctly. The driver is presumed to be the person who committed the offence unless the registered operator nominates another driver.

Demerit Points System

Full licence holders: Maximum 12 demerit points in any 3-year period before licence suspension.

Probationary drivers (P1/P2): Lower thresholds apply.

Learner drivers: Lower thresholds apply.

Demerit points accumulate and remain valid for 3 years. Speeding over 25 km/h does not attract demerit points but results in immediate licence suspension.
